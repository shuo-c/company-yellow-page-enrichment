# Industry Review Style Guide

## Goal
- Make translation fluent, professional, and concise.
- Preserve facts; do not invent certifications, awards, numbers, or claims.

## Tone
- Neutral-professional, business listing style.
- Prefer short declarative sentences.
- Avoid exaggerated marketing language.

## Must Preserve
- Company names (unless explicit localized name is provided)
- Proper nouns, addresses, phone numbers, emails, URLs
- Legal entities and registration strings

## Editing Rules
1. Fix literal translation that sounds unnatural in target language.
2. Keep meaning equivalent; do not add new business capabilities.
3. Use glossary translation when available.
4. Keep formatting stable for machine writeback.

## Chinese (zh-CN) specifics
- Use full-width punctuation where natural.
- End formal intro/description sentences with `。` when appropriate.
- Keep address/website/phone unmodified.
